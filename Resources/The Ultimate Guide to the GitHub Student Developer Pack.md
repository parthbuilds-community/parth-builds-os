# 🎓 The Ultimate Guide to the GitHub Student Developer Pack

**Stop paying for developer tools. Start building like a pro — for free.**

As a student, you already have enough expenses. The GitHub Student Developer Pack gives you **$200+ worth of professional developer tools, cloud credits, and learning resources** — completely free.

This guide will walk you through exactly how to get the Pack and what amazing benefits you unlock.

---

## ✅ Step 1: Check Your Eligibility

You can get the GitHub Student Developer Pack if you meet **all** of the following:

- ✅ You are currently enrolled in a **degree or diploma program** (high school, college, university, or bootcamp)
- ✅ You have a **verified student email address** (e.g., `.edu`, `.ac.XX`) or **proof of enrollment** (student ID, class schedule, enrollment letter)
- ✅ You are **at least 13 years old**
- ✅ You have a **personal GitHub account** (not an organization account)

> 💡 **Note:** There’s no “application fee” — it’s 100% free.

---

## 📝 Step 2: Prepare Your Documents

Before applying, gather **one** of these:

| Document Type | Examples |
|---------------|----------|
| School-issued email | `name@university.edu`, `name@college.ac.uk` |
| Student ID card | Photo showing your name, school, and expiration date (current) |
| Enrollment proof | Class schedule, tuition receipt, enrollment letter (with current date) |
| Official transcript | Showing your name, school, and current enrollment |

> ⚠️ **Pro tip:** Block out sensitive info (student ID numbers, birthdate) if you want — only your name and enrollment status matter.

---

## 🚀 Step 3: Apply for the Pack

1. **Sign in** to your personal GitHub account  
   👉 [github.com](https://github.com)

2. Go to the **GitHub Student Developer Pack** page  
   👉 [education.github.com/pack](https://education.github.com/pack)

3. Click **“Get the Pack”** (or “Get student benefits”)

4. Fill out the application form:
   - Select your **academic status** (Student)
   - Enter your **school-issued email** (or choose “I don’t have a school email”)
   - Upload your **proof of enrollment** (image or PDF)
   - Briefly describe **how you plan to use GitHub** (e.g., “class projects, open source contributions, personal portfolio”)

5. Click **“Submit”**

---

## ⏳ Step 4: Wait for Approval

- **Typical wait time:** A few hours to 2 weeks (usually 1–3 days)
- You’ll receive an **email confirmation** from GitHub
- Once approved, the benefits are **activated immediately** on your account

> 🔁 **Renewal:** The Pack lasts as long as you're a student. GitHub may ask you to re-verify once per year.

## 📌 Step 5: Activate Your Benefits

Once approved:

1. Visit the [Student Developer Pack dashboard](https://education.github.com/pack)
2. Browse the **“All offers”** section
3. Click **“Get offer”** next to any tool — you’ll be redirected to their signup page with the discount auto-applied
4. Use your **GitHub account** to log in to partner sites when possible

> 🔥 **Pro tip:** Start with the tools you need *now* (e.g., GitHub Copilot, a domain, or cloud credits). You don’t have to activate everything at once.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### ❓ How long does the Pack last?
Until you graduate. GitHub may ask you to re-verify once a year.

### ❓ Can I use these for commercial projects?
Yes — most offers allow you to build anything, including startups. Check each partner’s terms, but generally, student = personal/commercial use.

### ❓ What if I’m denied?
You can appeal with better proof of enrollment (e.g., a dated enrollment letter from your registrar).

### ❓ Can I get the Pack if I’m 13–17?
Yes, but with some limitations (e.g., Microsoft Azure for ages 13–17 has slightly different credits). The vast majority of benefits still work.

### ❓ Do I need a school email?
No. If you don’t have one, choose “I don’t have a school email” and upload your student ID or enrollment proof.
