# What Actually Worked in My Internship Hunt

## Customize Every Application
- **Resume:** Swap 2-3 bullet points to match job description
- **Cover letter:** Mention specific company projects/tech
- **Why it works:** Shows you read the posting and care
- **Tool:** Save a master resume, make copies per application

## Cold Outreach on LinkedIn
**Template structure:**
1. Short intro (I'm X studying Y at Z)
2. Why this company/role specifically
3. One relevant project/skill
4. Ask: Can I send my resume?

**Tips:**
- Connect with recruiters first
- Personalize the message
- Include GitHub link in profile

## Apply Early and Often
- **Timing matters:** First 50 applicants reviewed most carefully
- **Set alerts:** LinkedIn, Indeed, company sites
- **Volume matters:** Aim for 2-3 applications daily
- **Don't self-reject:** Apply even if you don't meet all requirements

## Follow-Up Strategy
- Wait 5-7 days after applying
- Email: "I applied on [date] for [role], wanted to check on status"
- Keep it brief and professional
- Shows persistence without being annoying

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Resume tailored to job description
- [ ] Cover letter mentions company specifically
- [ ] LinkedIn profile updated and professional
- [ ] GitHub has 2-3 pinned projects
- [ ] Calendar reminder to follow up in 1 week
